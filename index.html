Finding the percentage of a given quantity :

Finding the percentage of a given quantity means calculating a specific part of the total quantity, which is expressed as a percentage.

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Convert the Percentage to a Fraction: Begin by expressing the percentage as a fraction. This is done by writing the percentage value over 100.
    Example: 20% becomes 20100 , which simplifies to 15
  2. Multiply by the Given Quantity: Multiply the fraction you obtained from converting the percentage by the quantity for which you want to find the percentage. This multiplication gives you the part of the quantity that the percentage represents.

Example:

Find 20% of 50.

  • Convert 20% to a fraction: 20100 = 15
  • Multiply this fraction by the quantity: 15 × 50 = 10
  • Result: 20% of 50 is 10.

Expressing One Quantity as a Percentage of Another Quantity

When you express one quantity as a percentage of another, you are effectively describing how large or small the first quantity is relative to the second one, on a scale of 0 to 100. This helps in making meaningful comparisons and understanding relationships between different quantities.

Steps to Express One Quantity as a Percentage of Another:

  1. Divide the ‘Part’ by the ‘Whole’: Start by dividing the quantity you are interested in (the part) by the total or reference quantity (the whole). This gives you a decimal number.
  2. Convert to Percentage: Multiply the result from the division by 100 to convert it into a percentage. This final number represents the part as a percentage of the whole.

Example:

Suppose a class has 30 students, and 18 of them are girls. To find out what percentage of the class is girls:

  • Step 1: Divide the number of girls by the total number of students:1830 = 0.6 .
  • Step 2: Convert the decimal to a percentage:
    (0.6 × 100)% = 60%

This calculation tells you that girls make up 60% of the class.

Note: To streamline the process, you can directly convert the fraction 1830 to a percentage by multiplying by 100 in a single step.


Real-Life Examples of Percentage Usage

Percentages are a vital part of everyday life, providing a way to express quantities in a standardized form that makes comparison and calculation straightforward.
Example : Imagine you are organizing a technology conference and at the end of the event, you want to analyze the attendance demographics to better plan future events. You record that out of 200 attendees, 120 are male and 80 are female.

Calculate the Percentage of Male Attendees:

Total attendees = 200 Number of male attendees = 120 Percentage of male attendees = (120200 × 100)% = 60%

Calculate the Percentage of Female Attendees:

Number of female attendees = 80 Percentage of female attendees = (80200 × 100)% = 40%

The analysis reveals that 60% of the attendees are male and 40% are female, indicating a predominance of male participants at the conference. Armed with this insight, organizers can choose topics that cater to the interests of both groups, ensuring a more inclusive and engaging event for all attendees.
